From the Ops Desk

Building Momentum — Growth, Ground-Level Insights & Operational Depth

BY THE OPERATIONS TEAM

May 2026 was a month of meaningful progress. Two full disbursal cycles were completed — each introducing new partners, new borrowers, and new learnings. The team ran its first-ever 4-day T-Day format across both cycles, and the results were encouraging: faster turnarounds, better coordination, and sharper projections.

On the ground, a field audit in Lalitpur offered critical insights into portfolio risks and recovery pathways — a reminder that strong lending operations require eyes in the field, not just numbers on a screen. Internally, the team welcomed two new members, rolled out a performance-based projection approval system, and moved closer to launching the Borrower App.

Three new partners were onboarded — each bringing a distinct focus area, from aquaculture to agricultural federation lending to community welfare. Together they widen Rang De's reach into livelihoods that matter deeply to underserved communities.

May also reinforced a truth we continue to learn: operational rigour and human empathy are not opposites. The best outcomes — in collections, in partner management, in borrower engagement — emerge when both are present together.

"Every cycle is an opportunity — not just to disburse, but to deepen the relationship."

Disbursal Highlights

Cycle 1 — 2nd May: ₹88.5 Lakhs, 153 Borrowers

DISBURSAL DESK

May's first disbursal cycle ran on the 2nd of May under the newly introduced 4-Day T-Day format (1st–4th May 2026) — a significant operational milestone. This format compressed the disbursal window into a tighter, more coordinated execution sequence, and the team rose to the occasion.

A total of 153 borrowers received loans amounting to ₹88,52,590, with investments of ₹91,75,656 raised through the platform. 28 partner organisations participated, with 314 Social Investors backing the cycle against a projection of ₹1,11,35,500, yielding a projection accuracy of 82.4%.

Three partners made their inaugural disbursals this cycle — a milestone in our partner growth journey: Brahmaputra Krishi FPC (7 loans), Farm2Food Foundation (5 loans), and Maa Kamala Farmers PC (2 loans).

On the repayment method front, 36 loans went via UPI Auto-pay, 25 via Emandate, and 92 via manual payment — reflecting the diversity of digital readiness across our borrower base. A standout operational achievement: the profile creation-to-publishing turnaround time was just 0.42 days. The dominant sector this cycle was Retail stores with 33 loans.

Capacity Building & Partner Development

Three New Partners — Three New Frontiers

PARTNER ONBOARDING TEAM

New Partner Profiles

Diocesan Development & Welfare Society (DDWS)

Community Welfare, Women Empowerment & Sustainable Livelihoods — Since 1980

Mandated by the Roman Catholic Diocese of Allahabad and registered in 1980, DDWS has spent over four decades working toward sustainable, community-driven change for the marginalised. To date, the society has made more than 25,000 families self-dependent — a figure that speaks to both scale and depth of impact.

What makes DDWS stand out is its philosophy of co-creation. Rather than implementing top-down programmes, DDWS ensures community participation at every stage — from identifying problems to proposing solutions to executing plans. Physical and moral contributions from communities are built into every initiative.

DDWS runs programmes spanning women empowerment, child rights, anti-slavery advocacy, sustainable livelihoods, local self-governance, skill development, and stakeholder sensitisation. Their reach extends across social action centres, NGO partnerships, and government linkages.

The organisation's track record of accountability — to communities, donors, and partner organisations — makes them a strong fit for Rang De's model of responsible, relationship-centred lending. With their first disbursals made in the Cycle 2 of May, DDWS begins a new chapter of financial access for their borrower community.

— ✦ —

Manjha Technologies

Phygital Aquaculture Platform — Seeding, Feeding & Harvesting Farmer Prosperity

Incubated at the Anand Agriculture University Incubation Center (Agri & Food Business Incubator), Manjha Technologies operates a phygital platform designed for inland aquaculture farmers — one of India's most underserved agricultural segments. Their model supports farmers through all three critical stages: Seeding (stocking ponds with fish seeds), Feeding (nutrition support for growth), and Harvesting (facilitating market-linked sales).

The platform has achieved 17,000+ app downloads with 15 Village Level Entrepreneurs (VLEs) across UP, Bihar, Odisha, and Uttarakhand. Feed and healthcare have been provided to 300+ farmers, with an impressive 80% repeat order rate for feed — a strong signal of trust and product relevance.

Ecosystem Engagement

Technical partnerships with CIBA (Central Institute of Brackishwater Aquaculture) and NBFGR (National Bureau of Fish Genetic Resources) provide policy and farmer advisory support.

On-ground Aqua Consultants provide technical guidance, while 15 established VLEs serve as the last-mile delivery network.

Revenue Model

Revenue streams include margins on sales of medicines, fertilizers, and equipment, as well as listing charges from premium fish feed brands.

Rang De's partnership with Manjha Technologies opens a pathway to credit for aquaculture farmers — a community with real working capital needs and limited access to formal financial services.

— ✦ —

Praja Mitra Raithu MAC's Federation

Agricultural Working Capital & Input Finance — Utnoor, Telangana

Praja Mitra Raithu MAC's Federation operates in Utnoor, Adilabad district, Telangana. The federation serves member farmers focused on agricultural working capital and input costs — addressing credit needs that are seasonal, urgent, and often poorly served by formal channels.

A pilot has been discussed and is in progress. The partnership reflects Rang De's growing focus on agri-federation based lending models that serve farmers through collective structures with built-in accountability.

Trainings & Capacity Sessions

Building Capability Across the Partner Network

OPERATIONS TEAM

All three newly onboarded partners — DDWS, Manjha Technologies, and Praja Mitra Federation — underwent structured onboarding sessions to familiarise them with Rang De's processes, platform, and operational expectations. These sessions covered the end-to-end disbursal journey, repayment tracking, borrower profiling, and field documentation standards.

"New partners don't just learn our system — they start building their relationship with it."

In addition to new partner onboarding, refresher trainings were conducted for Manjari Foundation (across locations) and TRIF teams. These sessions focused on updating field staff on revised processes, reinforcing repayment discipline protocols, and reviewing borrower profiling quality — areas where even strong partners benefit from periodic alignment.

Training remains one of the highest-leverage investments in long-term portfolio quality. Partners who understand why Rang De does things the way it does — not just how — are more resilient when challenges arise in the field.

Ground Experiences

Field Audit — Lalitpur, June 2026

A deep-dive into overdue accounts reveals both risks and recovery pathways — conducted by Vedant.

— ✦ —

A comprehensive field audit was conducted in Lalitpur district, reviewing 36 overdue loan accounts — comprising 30 NPA accounts (90+ Days Past Due) and 6 watch-list accounts (31–60 DPD). The audit identified high-risk clusters, genuine hardship cases, and a set of accounts with actionable recovery potential.

Of the 36 accounts reviewed, 15 borrowers have provided payment commitments between June and July 2026 — creating a near-term recovery window that requires close monitoring and timely escalation.

🔴 High Risk  Five-Member Family Cluster — Five borrowers from the same family have all crossed 90+ DPD and were found actively avoiding contact. Combined overdue stands at approximately ₹1.45 lakh. Despite committing repayment by June 20, likelihood of recovery without intervention is low. Immediate legal notices recommended.

🔴 High Risk  Pulse/Dal Unit Loans — NPA — Five loans sanctioned for pulse/dal unit businesses have all slipped into NPA with combined outstanding exceeding ₹3 lakh. Potential gaps in repayment-capacity assessment at origination. Direct PM intervention recommended.

🟠 Hardship Cases  Write-Off Candidates — Two borrowers are genuine write-off candidates: one lost her shop in a fire, another is physically incapacitated post-surgery with the highest DPD in the portfolio at 637 days. Both referred to Credit Committee. Two additional medical hardship cases (cobra bite, cancer) have committed repayment plans and require empathetic monitoring.

Borrower App — Coming Soon

Empowering Borrowers with Direct Access to Their Loan Journey

TECHNOLOGY & OPERATIONS TEAM

The Rang De Borrower App is poised to be a significant operational efficiency driver. By giving borrowers direct access to their loan information, repayment status, payment history, and loan documents, the app reduces dependence on field staff for routine queries — freeing them to focus on what matters most: collections, relationship management, and borrower engagement.

As borrowers independently check their dues, EMI schedules, and download documents, the volume of routine support queries is expected to reduce meaningfully over time. This creates space for field teams to engage more deeply with borrowers facing genuine difficulties, rather than spending time on basic information provision.

"Transparency breeds accountability — and the Borrower App is built on exactly that principle."

What the App Enables

✦ Real-time visibility into loan details, repayment schedules, and dues

✦ Direct payment facilitation through the app interface

✦ Downloadable loan documents with guided password access

✦ Field staff support tool — verify details during visits in real time

Field staff will continue to play a critical role in driving initial adoption — helping borrowers install the app, navigate it for the first time, and complete their first payments. Over time, this hand-holding investment creates self-sufficient borrowers with stronger repayment awareness and discipline.

Overall, the app is expected to increase transparency, reduce operational dependency for basic loan information, and improve borrower awareness of repayment obligations — supporting a stronger, more informed credit culture at the last mile.

Upcoming Focus Areas

What We're Building Toward

Capacity Building Upgrades

Training modules for Field Staff and Partner Managers are being redesigned to be more interactive, scenario-based, and reflective of real challenges encountered in the field. The goal is deeper learning, not passive instruction.

Quarterly Disbursal Planning

Improved planning frameworks are being built to align disbursal cycles with quarterly targets — enabling more predictable capital deployment, better SI mobilisation, and reduced last-minute projections pressure on partners.

Internal Process Streamlining

Responsibilities across the internal team are being more clearly bifurcated across the Demand, Repayments, and Quality verticals — reducing overlap, improving accountability, and enabling faster decision-making at every stage of the loan lifecycle.
